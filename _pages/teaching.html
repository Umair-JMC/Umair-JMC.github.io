Teaching Philosophy

I believe that teaching is one of the most liberating and rewarding endeavors in academia. Teaching and transferring knowledge are central to an academic career, and I see teaching as a direct way to contribute to society. I strive to incorporate this approach into my teaching philosophy.

My approach to teaching involves a three-pronged strategy. First, a good teacher should motivate and inspire a thirst for learning in students. I aim to create an engaging classroom experience that generates intrigue, curiosity, and a desire to learn. While it can be challenging to ignite that spark of motivation in students, I believe it is our duty to strive for this goal. I achieve this by keeping my courses as interactive as possible and incorporating case and scenario-based learning.

Second, successful teachers link classroom experiences with real-life relevance. I ensure that students are taught industry and market-relevant content, enabling them to apply their learning in their professional lives. This includes imparting transferable skills that can be applied in various job dimensions and providing a solid foundation for further education if students choose to pursue it.

Finally, teachers must anchor their curricula to the current state of the field and the standards of similar courses worldwide. I take care to maximize the utility of time spent by students in my class by aligning my syllabus with contemporary standards and ensuring its relevance.

To summarize, my teaching philosophy is rooted in the belief that education is a transformative force that extends beyond the classroom. I aim to inspire students by igniting a genuine passion for learning, help them connect theoretical concepts to practical applications, all while maintaining a curriculum aligned with contemporary standards. Ultimately, I do my best to provide students with the knowledge, tools, and skills they need to succeed in their careers and contribute meaningfully to society. I strive to keep my classroom inclusive and engaging, providing a learning environment which helps every student reach their full potential. By rigorously following these principles, I hope to make a lasting impact on students’ academic and professional careers, nurturing a successful generation of practitioners and leaders in public policy and administration.

My teaching interests include public administration, public policy analysis, education policy, economics, statistics, microeconomic theory, econometrics, and quantitative as well as qualitative research design. Additionally, I am enthusiastic about teaching courses across the public policy and public administration domain.

Teaching is a learned skill, and I am in a constant state of learning to develop effective teaching and pedagogical skills. I have a strong commitment to teaching with academic rigor and promoting students’ learning in both general disciplines and my specialist area. My teaching experience in- cludes successfully instructing undergraduate courses, with positive feedback from both peers and students. Previously, I taught an undergraduate course (Entrepreneurship and Small Business Management) at UET Lahore in Spring 2015 and served as a teaching assistant for a graduate-level micro-econometrics course (PAF573) at Arizona State University. I believe that teaching and learning benefit significantly from incorporating technology and innovation within the classroom and overall learning environment. I am proficient in using technology and have expertise with subject-specific software (e.g., Stata, R) and online learning management systems such as Canvas, Blackboard, and Moodle. I employ blended teaching models, such as flipped classrooms, where learning materials are introduced before class, allowing classroom time to be used for a mix of traditional lectures, interactive exercises, and online lab modules. Additionally, I actively pursue professional development to stay current with digital tools and innovative teaching methods, ensuring a dynamic and engaging learning environment.

I regularly seek feedback and evaluate my teaching practices to maintain high standards of learning and incorporate new research and innovations. I will always strive to conduct my teaching in accordance with university policy, including assessment, course coordination, and course materials. I am fully capable and enthusiastic about designing curricula, supervising students, and taking on departmental responsibilities.
